1. Define terminals and non-terminals with examples
	Terminals
•	Terminals are the basic symbols of a grammar that cannot be replaced or expanded further.
•	They appear in the final string generated by the grammar.
•	Usually written in lowercase letters, keywords, or symbols.
Examples:
•	In arithmetic grammar: + , * , ( , ) , id
•	In grammar:
•	S → aSb | c
Terminals are: a, b, c
	Non-terminals
•	Non-terminals are symbols that represent groups of strings and can be replaced using grammar rules.
•	They help define the structure of the language.
•	Usually written in uppercase letters.
Examples:
•	S, E, T, F
•	In grammar:
•	S → aSb | c
Non-terminal is: S
