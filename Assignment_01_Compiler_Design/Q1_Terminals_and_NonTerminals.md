## v1. Define Terminals and Non-Terminals (With Examples)

### ✅ Terminals
- Terminals are the **basic symbols** of a grammar that **cannot be replaced or expanded** further.
- They **appear in the final string** generated by the grammar.
- Usually written in **lowercase letters**, **keywords**, or **symbols**.

#### Examples
- In arithmetic grammar:  
  `+`, `*`, `(`, `)`, `id`

- In the grammar:
S → aSb | c
**Terminals:** `a`, `b`, `c`

---

### ✅ Non-Terminals
- Non-terminals are symbols that **represent groups of strings**.
- They **can be replaced** using grammar production rules.
- They help define the **structure of the language**.
- Usually written in **uppercase letters**.

#### Examples
- `S`, `E`, `T`, `F`

- In the grammar:
S → aSb | c
**Non-terminal:** `S`
